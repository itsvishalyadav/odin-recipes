Recipe Name: Samosa

Description

Samosa is the triangle-shaped legend of the streets, the crispy code of chaos stuffed with spicy secrets. It’s not just a snack—it’s a warm hug and a spicy punch rolled in flaky golden armor. Bite into one and you unlock memories, childhood trauma, and probably a second samosa.

They say no one eats just one samosa. That’s not a challenge, it's a prophecy. It’s been the unofficial national currency for chai-time negotiations, wedding gossip, and exam results since time immemorial. Handle with care—it will burn your tongue and steal your heart.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour – For the crispy outer shell.
  • Boiled potatoes – Mashed chaos inside.
  • Green peas – Optional, but adds some class.
  • Spices (cumin, garam masala, chili powder) – For the internal fireworks.
  • Oil – For deep-frying your regrets to perfection.
  • Salt – Because bland samosas are a crime.

Steps:

  1. Make dough with flour, salt, and oil. Let it rest (like you should).
  2. Sauté mashed potatoes with spices and peas. Let flavors fight it out.
  3. Roll dough into circles, cut in half, and fold into cones.
  4. Stuff the cones with potato mix and seal them like top-secret files.
  5. Deep fry until golden and crispy. Ignore the oil judging you.
  6. Serve with chutney and pretend you’ll only eat one.
